Signs of Mental Disorders

In order to properly handle and treat mental disorders, it is first important to be able to recognize them. The following are some common signs that someone could be suffering from a mental disorder:

Changes in Mood
Social Withdrawal
Cognitive Changes
Changes in Behavior

Common Mental Disorders

Anxiety Disorders

Anxiety disorders are a group of mental health conditions characterized by excessive worry, fear, or unease. Common types include generalized anxiety disorder (GAD), panic disorder, and social anxiety disorder, and they can significantly interfere with daily life.

Read More

Depression

Depression is a mood disorder marked by persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and a lack of interest or pleasure in activities. It can affect a person's thoughts, behavior, and physical well-being, often leading to fatigue, changes in appetite, and difficulty concentrating.

Read More

Bipolar Disorder

Bipolar disorder, previously known as manic-depressive illness, involves extreme mood swings between periods of mania (elevated mood, energy) and depression. These episodes can vary in intensity and duration, impacting a person's ability to function in daily life.

Read More

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder

PTSD is a mental health condition that can develop after exposure to a traumatic event. Symptoms include intrusive memories, nightmares, hyperarousal, and avoidance behaviors, significantly affecting an individual's quality of life

Read More

Schizophrenia

Schizophrenia is a severe and chronic mental disorder characterized by disturbances in thinking, perception, emotions, and behavior. Individuals with schizophrenia may experience hallucinations, delusions, and impaired cognitive function.

Read More

Eating Disorders

Eating disorders, such as anorexia nervosa, bulimia nervosa, and binge-eating disorder, involve extreme attitudes and behaviors related to food, weight, and body image. They can have serious physical and emotional consequences.

Read More

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder

OCD is an anxiety disorder characterized by persistent, unwanted thoughts (obsessions) and repetitive behaviors or mental acts (compulsions). These rituals are often performed to alleviate the anxiety associated with obsessions.

Read More

Autism Spectrum Disorder

ASD is a neurodevelopmental disorder characterized by challenges in social interaction, communication difficulties, and repetitive behaviors. It encompasses a wide range of symptoms and levels of impairment, leading to a spectrum of abilities and challenges.

Read More

Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder

ADHD is a neurodevelopmental disorder that manifests in childhood and often continues into adulthood. It is characterized by persistent patterns of inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity, impacting academic, occupational, and social functioning.
